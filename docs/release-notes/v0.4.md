# v0.4, hnscc-time-multimodal

**Arm 4 — HPV± overall-survival stratification.** Adds the single strongest
clinical stratifier in head-and-neck cancer to the multimodal portrait, and
reports it honestly.

## What ships

### Arm 4, HPV± survival
- New module `hnscc_time.hpv`: Kaplan-Meier, log-rank, and univariate Cox on
  overall survival, grouped by molecular HPV status.
- New data source pinned in `data/manifest.yaml` under `hpv:` — every TCGA-HNSC
  case with a molecular HPV test result joined to overall survival
  (`tcga_hnsc/hpv_status.tsv`, n=110: 42 HPV+, 68 HPV-). HPV status is mapped
  from the GDC `follow_ups.molecular_tests` `test_result`
  (Positive/Amplified → positive; Negative/Not Amplified → negative). The TSV is
  canonicalized (rows sorted) and sha256-pinned; `make data` reproduces it
  byte-for-byte.
- New pipeline arm (Arm 4), independently optional and audit-bracketed like the
  others; writes `hpv/hpv_survival.json` + a Kaplan-Meier plot.

## The result (reported as-is)

**Cox HR 0.48 (95% CI 0.21–1.10) for HPV+ vs HPV-**, i.e. the *expected
protective direction* — but **trend-level, not significant** (log-rank
p = 0.076) in this HPV-tested subset.

This is the honest read, not a disappointment to hide: HPV's prognostic benefit
concentrates in **oropharyngeal** disease, and the TCGA-HNSC HPV-tested subset is
mixed-subsite and modest in size (few events among HPV+). The direction and
effect size match the established literature; the significance bar is not cleared
at this cohort. The deliverable is a correctly-specified, reproducible survival
comparison that states its own power limits.

## Scope / honesty boundary

- Arm 4 is **clinical survival only**. A multimodal HPV × TIME-profile comparison
  would need cases with *both* HPV status and STAR counts; the random n=50
  genomics subset overlaps the HPV-tested set in only ~10 cases (3 HPV+), too few
  to claim anything — so it is deliberately **not** attempted here.
- Median OS can read counter-intuitively under heavy censoring (HPV+ has far
  fewer events); the Cox HR + event counts are the proper summary and are what we
  report.

## Reproducibility / tests

- `make data` fetches + sha256-verifies the HPV table; `make run` runs Arm 4 and
  skips gracefully if the table is absent.
- `tests/test_hpv.py` plants a strong synthetic HPV+ advantage and asserts the
  summary recovers a protective, significant HR; `tests/test_manifest.py` guards
  the pinned `hpv:` block.
- New dependency: `lifelines` (pulls `matplotlib`).
